## ✅ **1. Currency – How timely is the information?**

Ask:

* When was the blog post published or last updated?
* Are the links within the post still active?
* Is the topic one where up-to-date information matters (e.g., technology, health, current events)?
* Does the blog show signs of being maintained?

What to look for:

* Visible publication/update dates
* Recent references
* No broken links

---

## ✅ **2. Relevance – How useful is this information for your needs?**

Ask:

* Does the content actually address your topic or question?
* Is it written at the appropriate level (too basic, too advanced)?
* Is the information complete enough?
* Is it intended for the correct audience (students, professionals, general readers)?

What to look for:

* Clear topic focus
* Information that matches your purpose (research, decision-making, learning)

---

## ✅ **3. Authority – Who is behind the content?**

Ask:

* Who is the author? Are they identified clearly?
* What are the author’s credentials, background, or experience?
* Is the blog associated with a reputable organization?
* Can you find contact information or author transparency?

What to look for:

* Author bio
* Links to professional profiles
* Clear ownership of the website

---

## ✅ **4. Accuracy – How correct and reliable is the content?**

Ask:

* Does the blog provide sources, data, citations, or evidence?
* Can the content be verified through other trustworthy sources?
* Is the writing free of obvious errors or contradictions?
* Does the author distinguish between facts and opinions?

What to look for:

* Citations or references
* External links to credible sources
* Balanced, evidence-supported statements

---

## ✅ **5. Purpose – Why does this blog exist?**

Ask:

* Is the blog trying to inform, persuade, entertain, or sell something?
* Is there an obvious bias or agenda?
* Are ads, affiliate links, or sponsored content influencing the information?
* Is the tone objective or opinion-driven?

What to look for:

* Disclosure statements
* Neutral vs. promotional tone
* Transparency about motives

---

## 🎯 **How to use CRAAP in practice**

1. Read the blog post once normally.
2. Reread it and go through each CRAAP category with the above questions.
3. Assign a score (e.g., 1–5) for each criterion if you want a structured assessment.
4. Summarize your findings:

   * Which criteria does the blog meet strongly?
   * Where are the weaknesses?
5. Decide if the blog is **credible enough** for your intended use.
